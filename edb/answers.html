Dog Ownership Readiness Quiz Answers

How well did you do? Check your answers and learn more about dog care.


1. You are baking cookies when a chocolate chip falls onto the floor. Before you can pick it up, a large yellow lab runs over and eats the chocolate chip. You...

  • Rush the dog to the vet
  • Yell at the dog until it gets scared
  • Move the bag farther from the edge and continue baking
  • Feed the dog more chocolate

A single chocolate chip is unlikely to do any real harm to a big dog. This is a tiny mistake and you don't need to worry about it.

2. You're making a salad, and your dog begs for food. What would be OK to give your dog?

  • Spinach
  • Carrots
  • Macadamia nuts
  • Cucumbers

Spinach, carrots, and cucumbers are healthy for dogs. But macadamia nuts are poisonous to them.

3. Which of these things should you do first in the morning?

  • Take the dog out
  • Feed the dog
  • Get yourself ready for the day
  • Check your phone

After a long night indoors, dogs need to stretch their legs and relieve themselves. Handle your usual routine after letting out the dog.

4. Do dogs typically thrive on a vegetarian diet?

  • Yes
  • No

It is very hard to keep a dog healthy on a vegetarian diet. Vegetarian dogs may experience health complications such as seizures. While many vegetables are good for dogs, dogs are healthiest when they eat meat too.

If you want a vegan or vegetarian pet, try a rabbit or a rodent such as a hamster or gerbil.

5. How much guacamole can you give your dog?

  • Plenty
  • Just a little
  • None

Avocado is toxic to dogs, so your dog should not eat guacamole.

6. Can you let your dog off its leash in any field?

  • Yes
  • No

There are several things you need to consider before letting your dog off leash:

  • Environment safety: Are there snakes, broken glass, or busy streets nearby?
  • Prey drive: Does your dog like to chase local animals? They may run into the street or woods.
  • Recall and training: How well-trained is your dog right now? If you say "come" or "leave it," will they listen (even with interesting distractions)?
  • Strangers: Some people are scared of dogs. They may feel scared or react badly if your dog is off leash. Be cautious around people you don't know.
  • Rules: Obey signs and local laws.

Stick to familiar, safe places if you're going to take your dog off leash. Using a long lead may also be a good option if you want to give your dog more freedom while keeping them safe.
