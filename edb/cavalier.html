King Charles Cavalier Spaniel

Cavalier Spaniel
Breed Size Energy Kid-Friendly Grooming Needs
Cavalier Spaniel small moderate yes low

Cavalier King Charles Spaniel dogs are known for their gentle, sweet temperaments. They're popular therapy dogs due to their love of people. Affectionate and cuddly, they're likely to follow their owners around and look for a chance to sit in your lap. They do like to chase birds and squirrels, and are best kept on a leash outdoors, especially near traffic.

Cavalier spaniels are adaptable, and get along well with both active owners and homebodies. They can live happily in an apartment or house. Thanks to their easygoing temperaments, they usually do well with kids. Their intelligence makes training easier, and they respond well to positive reinforcement. (Harsh words, however, may cause them to hide or give up.) They can get lonely if left alone for too long, so an attentive owner is best.

Traits of a Cavalier

  • Small size: Cavalier spaniels are usually only a foot tall.
  • Moderate energy: Cavaliers can adapt to different lifestyles. They're happy to go on long hikes or just sit on your lap.
  • Kid friendliness: Cavaliers get along well with older kids. Toddlers and young kids may play too roughly with a cavalier.
  • Minor grooming needs: Cavaliers need to be brushed two or three times a week. They don't drool, and shedding is moderate.
