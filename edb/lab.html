Labrador Retriever

Labrador Retriever

Social and active, labs are the most popular dogs in America. Known for their friendliness, labs are eager to play with people of all ages. They enjoy high-energy outdoor activities like swimming and long games of fetch. They are highly adaptable as long as they get sufficient exercise.

Labs do best in a home with a backyard, but can adapt to smaller homes as long as they get enough exercise. Thanks to an easygoing nature, they get along well with other dogs and kids of all ages. Labs can get lonely, so they're happiest in an attentive family. Be careful about cleanliness during adolescence: around ages 2-4, labs may swallow objects like toys, socks, and rocks.

Traits of a Labrador Retriever

  • Medium-large size: A lab is usually around or just under 2 feet tall.
  • High energy: Labs are one of the most active breeds, needing intense daily exercise.
  • Kid friendliness: Labs are excellent with kids.
  • Low grooming needs: While grooming is easy, labs do shed a lot and drool moderately.
